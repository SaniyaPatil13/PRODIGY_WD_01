Principles for a healthy life.

Tridosha (Three Doshas):

Vata, Pitta, and Kaphaâ€”which are the fundamental energies that govern various physiological and psychological functions in the body. These doshas represent different combinations of the five elements (Earth, Water, Fire, Air, and Ether). Maintaining a balance among the doshas is essential for overall health, while imbalances are believed to contribute to diseases. Understanding and managing one's unique doshic constitution (Prakriti) is a central aspect of Ayurvedic health care.

Dinacharya (Daily Routine):

Ayurveda emphasizes the importance of a daily routine (Dinacharya) aligned with the natural rhythms of the day. This includes practices such as waking up early, cleansing rituals, exercise, meditation, and regular meal times. Following a consistent daily routine is believed to promote balance, enhance digestion, and support overall well-being.

Ahara (Dietary Guidelines):

Ayurvedic dietary principles focus on consuming a balanced and wholesome diet tailored to one's doshic constitution and the current state of balance or imbalance. The six tastes (sweet, sour, salty, bitter, pungent, and astringent) are considered in meal planning to ensure a variety of nutrients and promote satisfaction. Ayurveda emphasizes mindful eating, considering not only what is eaten but also how, when, and where it is consumed.
